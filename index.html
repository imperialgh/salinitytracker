    The Coastal Life-Line Project

    Background: South-Western Bangladesh (Satkhira/Khulna) faces a 10x global average sea level rise. 60% of drinking water is contaminated by salinity, causing gestational hypertension and stunting.

    The Mission: Addressing the "Salt-Nutritional Crisis" by implementing decentralized water and food security solutions that use local materials and knowledge.

    1. Salinity Intrusion: Forces freshwater scarcity.
    2. Agri-Transition: Rice paddies to shrimp ponds.
    3. Dietary Gaps: Loss of Potassium ($K^+$) rich greens.
    4. Health Outcome: 31.8% Gestational Hypertension.
    NEW PROTOTYPE: BIOMIMETIC

    CocoShield™ Seed Encapsulation

    A coconut-inspired multi-layer capsule (Pectin + Potassium Alginate) that buffers seed hydration during saline germination. Enables the growth of Okra and Amaranth in high-salt soil.

    Impact: Restore $K^+$ and $Fe$ levels in diet.

    Scale: Local bazaar materials.

    Uses gravity to inject filtered monsoon rain into salty aquifers, creating a "Freshwater Bubble" for irrigation and drinking.
